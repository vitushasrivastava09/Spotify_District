01 — The Partnership

The model: book courts, earn music

District-led, Spotify-rewarded. Users book football turf slots via District Play. After 3–4 completed bookings, they unlock 2–3 months of Spotify Premium free — no upfront cost, no friction.

Why District-led, not Spotify-led: Spotify's marginal cost of gifting Premium months is near zero — it's a high-perceived-value reward that costs almost nothing to distribute. District gets the KPI it needs (repeat booking frequency) while Spotify gets distribution into an offline-active, premium-urban audience it can't easily reach through digital funnels. Each brand gives what is cheap for it and valuable to the other's user.
02 — The Insight

Two trends converging at the perfect moment

The hypothesis is backed by research — and the timing is right. India's Gen Z is heading back outside, football fandom is surging, and the World Cup has no Indian broadcaster. Fan energy has nowhere to go. The pitch is the outlet.

Global research
The offline renaissance is real
Gen Z and Millennials are the loneliest generations on record despite being the most digitally connected — 67% of Gen Z and 65% of Millennials reported loneliness (Cigna/Evernorth, n=7,500+, 2024). Human8's "What Matters 2025" found 73% of Gen Z report digital exhaustion, driving a deliberate pivot to in-person "third places." Snapchat × Portas Agency (YouGov, 2,000+ Gen Z, 2026) confirmed 51% prefer in-person over digital equivalents.

India · Live events boom
India's youth is going out — fast
India's live events industry hit ₹17,000 crore in 2025, with 70% of attendees under 35 (EY-Parthenon). Turf and participatory sport are surging — pickleball courts grew 6× in 18 months. Football is India's #2 sport with 138.7M fans (Nielsen, 2024), concentrated in the 18–35 band. FIFA World Cup 2026 kicked off June 11 with no Indian broadcaster — fan energy is unspent. 03 & 04 — The Win-Win

Why it works for both sides

This isn't a sponsorship or a logo-swap. Each brand solves a specific problem the other uniquely helps with.

Spotify
The conversion engine Spotify needs in India
  • India is Spotify's highest-growth, lowest-ARPU market — free-to-paid conversion is the core challenge, not awareness.
  • District Play users are the premium-urban-young cohort hardest to reach through standard digital funnels — this partnership puts Spotify exactly there.
  • A 2–3 month Premium runway is long enough to build the playlist and algorithm habit that drives long-term retention past the promo period.
  • Globally proven playbook: Spotify × FC Barcelona (Camp Nou naming), EA Sports FC 25 integration, Starbucks cross-loyalty. This is the India-native version.
D
District
The repeat-booking catalyst District needs
  • District Play is live in Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Pune, Hyderabad and Jaipur — but booking frequency is the KPI the Play vertical needs to build.
  • Spotify Premium is the ideal reward: high perceived value, zero inventory cost to District, loved by exactly the 18–35 demographic using District Play.
  • Requiring 3–4 bookings in a defined window ensures incremental frequency, not a subsidy for bookings that would have happened anyway.
  • Co-branded "Football Season" campaign during World Cup 2026 gives District a cultural narrative that goes beyond utility — it's a moment.
  • District already accounts for 20% of Eternal's business (Deepinder Goyal, 2025). This accelerates the Play vertical's path to scale.
